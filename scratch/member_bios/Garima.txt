Garima Srivastava is a PhD in Rural Sociology at the University of Missouri, with an interdisciplinary background spanning Environmental Planning and Architecture. Her research focuses on the intersection of agriculture, sustainability, and social equity.
Guided by a deep spiritual path and devotion to her Aradhya, she champions a life of minimalism and sustainability. She views faith not as a retreat, but as a practical methodology—using spiritual principles to drive collective, action-oriented solutions to social and material challenges. For her, the WISE is the vital bridge between her inner conviction and the purposeful use of resources, serving as her ultimate pathway to lasting bliss.
