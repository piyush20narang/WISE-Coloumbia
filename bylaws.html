Corporate Bylaws

Official rules and regulations of WISE, Columbia, Inc.

BYLAWS OF

WISE, Columbia, Inc.

A Missouri Nonprofit Corporation

ARTICLE ONE - NAME AND PURPOSE

Name: 1.01. The name of this Corporation is WISE, Columbia, Inc. (the "Corporation").

Purpose: 1.02. The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, religious, and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. The specific purposes for which this Corporation is organized are:

  1. To highlight the importance of Spiritual Education for Self-Realization that empowers and harmonizes one’s thoughts, words, and actions towards the higher purpose of humanity.
  2. Redefining success with mindful and purposeful community goals and achievements.
  3. To establish a system for spiritual education that transforms the service attitude with values of love and gratitude.
  4. To bring the members closer together for the purpose of living a simpler and more natural way of life.

ARTICLE TWO - OFFICES AND AGENCY

Principal and Branch Offices: 2.01. The principal place of business of this Corporation will be in Missouri. In addition, the Corporation may maintain other offices either within or without the State of Missouri as its business requires.

Location of Registered Office: 2.02. The location of the initial registered office of this Corporation will be in Missouri. Such office will be continuously maintained in the State of Missouri for the life of this Corporation.

ARTICLE THREE - MEMBERSHIP

Membership: 3.01. The founding member(s) of this Corporation shall be (hereinafter “the Membership”), or any successor thereto.

General Members: The Corporation may have General Members who support the mission and purposes of the organization. The Board of Directors shall establish the qualifications, rights, and duties of General Members.

ARTICLE FOUR - BOARD OF DIRECTORS

Management: 4.01. The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by its Board of Directors.

Number and Qualifications: 4.02. The Board of Directors shall consist of at least three (3) but no more than nine (9) individuals.
