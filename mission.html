About Us

Our Mission, Vision, and Core Values

Our Mission

  • To highlight the importance of Spiritual Education for Self-Realization that empowers and harmonizes one’s thoughts, words, and actions towards the higher purpose of humanity.

  • Redefining success with mindful and purposeful community goals and achievements.

  • To establish a system for spiritual education that transforms the service attitude with values of love and gratitude.

Our Vision

  • To build a strong community working with co-operation for shared goals with foundations of spiritual values.

  • To develop a self sustained resilient community by developing a sustainable agrifood system which is physically, mentally, socially, economically, spiritually and environmentally aligned.

  • Realization of Divinity and Utilization of the Gifts of Nature to serve the community for harmonious living.

Core Values

The Core values of WISE are inspired and guided by following Principles in Vedic Spiritual Scriptures.

"We strive towards Purposeful goals with Integrity, Compassion, Patience, Enthusiasm, Determination, Purity, Service attitude and Gratitude AS IT IS described in Vedic Scriptures:"

Bhagavad Gita, Verse (12.13 & 12.14)

adveṣṭā sarva-bhūtānāṁ
maitraḥ karuṇa eva ca
nirmamo nirahaṅkāraḥ
sama-duḥkha-sukhaḥ kṣamī

santuṣṭaḥ satataṁ yogī
yatātmā dṛḍha-niścayaḥ
mayy arpita-mano-buddhir
yo mad-bhaktaḥ sa me priyaḥ

"One who is not envious but is a kind friend to all living entities, who does not think himself a proprietor and is free from false ego, who is equal in both happiness and distress, who is tolerant, always satisfied, self-controlled, and engaged in devotional service with determination, one’s mind and intelligence fixed on Me – such a devotee of Mine is very dear to Me."

Nectar of Instruction, Text 3

utsāhān niścayād dhairyāt
tat-tat-karma-pravartanāt
saṅga-tyāgāt sato vṛtteḥ
ṣaḍbhir bhaktiḥ prasidhyati

"Six principles favorable to the execution of pure devotional service: (1) being enthusiastic, (2) endeavoring with confidence, (3) being patient, (4) acting according to regulative principles, (5) association of like minded individuals, and (6) following in the footsteps of the previous ācāryas. These six principles undoubtedly assure the complete success of pure devotional service."
